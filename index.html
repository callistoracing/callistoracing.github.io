Innovating for the F1 in Schools Competition

Overview

Callisto Racing is an ambitious and innovative racing team competing in the prestigious F1 in Schools competition. Our team of dedicated secondary school students is passionate about engineering, design, and the thrill of motorsport. We are committed to pushing the boundaries of performance and creativity to achieve excellence both on and off the track.

Callisto Racing was chosen as it represents a modern, sci-fi sound that fits an engineering team. It is unique, easy to remember, and important for branding.

Meaning and Story Behind the Name

Callisto, in mythology, was a huntress associated with Artemis, known for agility and sharp instincts, aligning with high-performance engineering.

Our Mission

To design, manufacture, and race a miniature Formula 1 car that combines cutting-edge engineering principles, aerodynamic efficiency, and striking visual design. Through teamwork and collaboration, we aim to deliver a car that excels on the track while embodying the spirit of F1 innovation.

Our Strategy

  • Innovation in Design: Using advanced CAD software and wind tunnel simulations to refine every curve and component of our car.
  • Precision Manufacturing: Leveraging CNC machining and 3D printing for a high-quality, lightweight build.
  • Sustainable Practices: Incorporating eco-friendly materials and minimizing waste in our processes.
  • Community Engagement: Building partnerships with sponsors, promoting STEM education, and inspiring the next generation of innovators.

Communications

Having good communication within a team is a vital part of a competition like this. We prioritised having good communication links with each other as well as our sponsors, allowing us to work through any issue we may face collectively.

By creating a team page, we are able to share thoughts and ideas, as well as updates on our individual progress with the rest of the team. It also provides a collective space to store important documents for everyone to access.

Our group meets once a week to discuss our progress and work through any issues that may have arisen during the week. Meeting face-to-face allows better communication, as well as helping others with different aspects of the competition.

If an urgent message needs to be sent during the week, we may call each other to make decisions or work through a problem.
